Blockchain and cryptocurrency have evolved from a cryptographic experiment in 2009 into a multi-trillion-dollar asset class, an active area of research, and a regulatory challenge spanning every major jurisdiction. The journey has not been linear: it has been marked by large booms and busts, by the rise and fall of exchanges and protocols, by the entry of the world's largest financial institutions into crypto markets, and by the gradual, patchy construction of a regulatory framework.

I have just completed a comprehensive research brief that examines this entire arc. Drawing on verified market data, regulatory sources, corporate filings, and a systematic review of 27 academic papers in ABS 4/4* and ABDC A* journals, the report provides an evidence-based analysis focused on corporate adoption, accounting and reporting, regulation, and systemic risk.

## What the brief covers

The report is organised around ten sections following an academic structure:

- **Technology evolution**: From Bitcoin's proof-of-work to Ethereum's smart contracts and proof-of-stake, covering enterprise and consortium blockchains, and the lessons from TradeLens's failure
- **Market development and cycles**: Four boom-bust cycles culminating in a $4.31 trillion peak in October 2025, with analysis of the 2022 cascade of exchange and protocol failures
- **Corporate adoption**: Strategy's 843,775 BTC treasury (verified against SEC 8-K filings), Tesla, Block, Marathon Digital, and the accelerating involvement of banks and payment providers
- **Accounting and auditing**: FASB ASU 2023-08's shift from impairment-only to fair value measurement, IFRS treatment gaps, and the emerging practice of proof of reserves
- **Global regulation**: EU MiCA as the first comprehensive framework, US fragmentation and ETF approvals, CBDC progress across 146 countries, and FATF Travel Rule implementation
- **Systemic risks**: Volatility, custody, fraud, energy consumption, and contagion between crypto and traditional finance
- **Hot themes**: Tokenisation of real-world assets, stablecoins as payments infrastructure ($312 billion market cap), and institutional adoption via ETFs ($51.3 billion in Bitcoin ETF inflows)
- **Research opportunities**: Accounting for crypto assets, corporate treasury management, regulatory effects on market quality, DeFi governance, and blockchain audit and assurance

## Key findings

The brief incorporates 11 interactive ECharts visualisations and 8 downloadable datasets with documented methodology. Among the findings:

- Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) holds 843,775 BTC at a cost basis of $63.7 billion ($75,476 per BTC) as of 6 July 2026, verified against SEC 8-K filings
- US spot Bitcoin ETFs, approved 10 January 2024, have accumulated $51.3 billion in net inflows; spot Ether ETFs ($11.0 billion) represent 4.57 percent of Ether's circulating market cap
- The 2022 cascade of failures (Terra/LUNA, 3AC, Celsius, FTX) destroyed over $100 billion in market value, but all were failures of centralised intermediaries, not blockchain protocols
- FASB ASU 2023-08 replaced the impairment-only cost model with fair value measurement, removing the single largest accounting deterrent to corporate crypto holdings
- 146 countries representing over 98 percent of global GDP are exploring CBDCs; 13 cross-border wholesale CBDC projects are active
- Stablecoins have emerged as the most commercially successful blockchain application, reaching $312 billion in market capitalisation and integrating into Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, and Stripe payment rails
